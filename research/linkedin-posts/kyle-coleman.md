# Kyle Coleman — LinkedIn Posts Collection

## Post 1: The Best Two Cold Emails I've Received
**Date**: Recurring theme (originally 2021, updated takes through 2025)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/kyletcoleman/

**Summary**: Coleman shared the best cold emails he received while responding to every inbound cold email for several weeks. What made them stand out:
- Great research with a clear tie-in to their value proposition
- Short, easy-to-read sentences with plain language
- Plenty of white space for visual scannability
- Strong calls to action

He took meetings with both senders. His analysis highlights that good cold email is about signal density — every sentence earns its place.

**Notable Debate**: A commenter disagreed — arguing the emails were too long, too ingratiating, and that personal details (knowing someone's dog breed) feel invasive rather than personalized. Coleman acknowledged the tension: personalization has a line where it becomes uncomfortable.

## Post 2: Cold Email 101 Webinar (1,400+ Registrants)
**Date**: 2022
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kyletcoleman_cold-email-101

**Summary**: Framework from Coleman's webinar covering:
- Common cold email mistakes (feature-dumping, weak CTAs, no social proof)
- His approach to gifting in outreach — reframing from "here's a $50 gift card for YOUR time" to "here's a $50 gift card you can send to someone on your team who did something great." The reframe creates goodwill and memorability even if it doesn't immediately book a meeting.
- RevOps as an underappreciated cold email audience

**Key Insight**: Most cold email advice focuses on writing. Coleman's emphasis is on the *offer* — what you're actually bringing to the table matters more than how you phrase it.

## Post 3: AI Experiments Across the Marketing Org
**Date**: 2026
**Source**: LinkedIn (at ClickUp)

**Summary**: Coleman describes mandating weekly AI experiment submissions from his marketing team. For 6 straight weeks, he personally reviewed every submission on Friday nights and Saturday mornings. Results: AI usage across the team increased 20x. His takeaway: adoption requires visible leadership investment, not just a Slack channel announcement.

**Relevance to Cold Outreach**: The same principle applies to adopting AI in outbound — tools like Copy.ai, Lavender, or Clay only work if the team builds a habit of using them. Top-down engagement drives adoption.
