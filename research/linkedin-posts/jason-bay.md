# Jason Bay — LinkedIn Posts Collection

## Post 1: Outbound Is Broken
**Date**: 2025–2026 (recurring theme)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/jasondbay/

**Summary**: Bay's central thesis: the Predictable Revenue model sold a lie. SDRs do outbound because it's "how you cut your teeth in sales" — not because it's strategic. The result: outbound has never been treated as more than random tactics (cheesy cold call openers, break-up emails, "keep dialing"). Meanwhile:
- Unknown callers go straight to voicemail or AI gatekeepers
- Emails land in spam
- The industry celebrates <2% reply rates and <4% cold call pick-up rates

The tools designed to make outbound easier have made it exponentially harder by enabling spray-and-pray at scale.

**Key Insight**: "Your org spends millions on sales methodologies, tech to help win deals, and meetings every week to review deals." But zero investment in how those deals enter the pipeline. The prospecting layer is an afterthought.

## Post 2: The REPLY Method
**Date**: 2025
**Source**: https://outboundsquad.com/

**Summary**: Bay's framework for cold email that generates double-digit reply rates:
- **R**esearch: Identify triggers and relevance before writing
- **E**mpathy: Lead with the prospect's world, not yours
- **P**ersonalization: Beyond first name — reference specific business context
- **L**ength: Keep under 90 words for first touch
- **Y**ield: Measure reply quality, not just reply quantity

**Key Insight**: Most teams optimize for volume metrics (emails sent, opens). Bay argues the only metric that matters is "qualified meetings per 100 emails sent."

## Post 3: The Offer — Why No One Responds to Cold Outreach
**Date**: 2026
**Source**: Outbound Squad Podcast

**Summary**: Bay identifies three offer types that actually book meetings:
1. **The Audit Offer**: "I found 3 things on your website that might be hurting conversion — want me to walk you through them?" (Specific, free value)
2. **The Comparison Offer**: "We just helped [competitor] do X — curious how you're approaching it?" (Leverages competitive instinct)
3. **The Insight Offer**: Share a non-obvious data point relevant to their role (earns credibility through expertise)

Most reps default to a fourth type — the Demo Offer ("want to see a demo?") — which has the lowest conversion rate because it asks for commitment before delivering value.
