# Michel Lieben — LinkedIn Posts Collection

## Post 1: The Tiered Personalization Strategy
**Date**: 2026
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/michellieben/ / ColdIQ blog

**Summary**: ColdIQ's approach to scaling personalization without sacrificing quality:
- **Tier 1 (Top accounts)**: One custom prompt per account, written by Claude or Clay using signal context. Full multi-channel: cold call + LinkedIn + email.
- **Tier 2 (Mid-priority)**: Templated hooks with one variable swap. LinkedIn + email only.
- **Tier 3 (Volume)**: High-volume sequences with automated personalization. Email only.

Channel intensity matches the tier. Every reply, meeting, and closed-won deal syncs back to CRM to improve future targeting.

**Key Insight**: "The 30 deals already in your pipeline are the ICP." Don't start with theory — start with who's already buying and build look-alike targeting from there.

## Post 2: Turning 24 Employees Into LinkedIn Influencers
**Date**: 2026
**Source**: ColdIQ blog

**Summary**: ColdIQ turned their entire team into content creators in 90 days:
- Quarterly internal challenge with cash prizes
- Full-time designers and content tools to remove friction
- 1:1 and group coaching with external LinkedIn experts
- Hiring people who already wanted to build their personal brand
- CEO (Lieben) leading by example: 60+ posts per quarter

Results: 581 posts, 34,023 new followers, $151K in new MRR, 27 new clients. Combined team audience exceeded 250K followers.

**Relevance to Cold Outreach**: Their sales team heard the same line: "we see you guys everywhere." Content became their best outbound channel — warming prospects before the cold email even lands.

## Post 3: Failed 7 Businesses Before ColdIQ
**Date**: 2025
**Source**: ColdIQ blog

**Summary**: Lieben's path to $7M ARR: each failed business taught one specific skill that compounded into success. The skills: paid acquisition, branding, affiliate monetization, technical ability, client delivery, and market timing. The lesson: a cold outreach agency requires a stack of skills, not just one.
