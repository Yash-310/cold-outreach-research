# Jed Mahrle — LinkedIn Posts Collection

## Post 1: Typos Actually Help Cold Emails
**Date**: 2023 (widely cited through 2025)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/outboundsales/

**Summary**: Mahrle sent 173 automated emails with the typo "oticed..." instead of "noticed..." and measured a 6% reply rate (mostly positive) compared to 3% from similar sequences with correct spelling and the same messaging. His conclusion: small typos and grammatical errors signal "human" rather than "automated" to recipients. In an inbox full of AI-polished emails, imperfection stands out.

**Key Insight**: This is counterintuitive but backed by data. As AI-generated emails become the norm, human signals (typos, casual phrasing, imperfect formatting) may become differentiators.

## Post 2: Email Deliverability Series
**Date**: 2022–2025 (ongoing)
**Source**: LinkedIn

**Summary**: Mahrle polled 1,000+ people and found nearly two-thirds reported cold email open rates below 40%. His argument: 60%+ is doable regardless of industry or volume. The problem isn't subject lines — it's deliverability infrastructure:
- Fix DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) before worrying about copy
- Warm domains slowly (start at 10-15 emails/day, scale over 2-3 weeks)
- Monitor blacklists actively
- Use separate domains for cold outreach (never your primary corporate domain)

**Key Insight**: "Writing better subject lines, preview lines, and A/B testing only solves half the problem. The other half is far less talked about: email deliverability."

## Post 3: Digital Sales Rooms as a Prospecting Tool
**Date**: 2023
**Source**: LinkedIn

**Summary**: Mahrle uses "digital sales rooms" (personal landing pages for prospects) in two ways:
1. **Mid-Funnel**: Keeps communication, next steps, and resources in one place for active deals
2. **Top-of-Funnel**: Acts as a lead magnet by providing genuinely helpful resources related to the problem he solves

He hyperlinks digital sales rooms in the P.S. of all cold emails, then tracks who clicks and what content they engage with. This enables personalized follow-ups based on actual engagement signals rather than guesswork.

## Post 4: Using ChatGPT for ICP Segmentation
**Date**: 2023
**Source**: LinkedIn

**Summary**: Mahrle's workflow for generating hyper-specific outbound segments:
1. Describe your company, product, and current customers to ChatGPT
2. Ask for 10 specific targeting ideas for your ICP
3. Filter for ideas that are new to you (not the obvious segments you're already hitting)
4. Build separate sequences for each micro-segment with tailored messaging

**Key Insight**: AI doesn't replace outbound strategy — it multiplies it. The value is in generating targeting angles you wouldn't think of, not in writing the emails themselves.
