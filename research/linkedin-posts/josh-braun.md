# Josh Braun — LinkedIn Posts Collection

## Post 1: The 4T Framework for Cold Emails
**Date**: 2024 (framework is ongoing)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/josh-braun/

**Summary**: Braun's signature framework addresses the four things a prospect unconsciously evaluates when opening a cold email:
1. **Trigger**: Why are you reaching out NOW? Reference a specific event — a job posting, a funding round, a product launch, a LinkedIn post. This answers "why me, why now?"
2. **Think**: Make the prospect think about a problem they might not be actively considering. Plant a seed of doubt. "Companies hiring 5+ SDRs often find that ramp time eats 40% of their first-year ROI."
3. **Third-Party Credibility**: Don't say "we're great." Say "Company X saw Y result." Social proof from a peer is 10x more persuasive than a self-claim.
4. **Talk**: End with a low-pressure CTA. Not "book a call." Instead: "Would you be opposed to learning more?" The negative framing ("opposed to") paradoxically increases positive responses.

**Key Insight**: The framework works because it shifts focus from what you're selling to what they care about. Each element answers a buyer's internal question.

## Post 2: Are You Over-Personalizing Cold Emails?
**Date**: 2020 (still widely referenced)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/josh-braun/

**Summary**: Braun argues that "personalization" has become a trap. Reps spend 15 minutes per email researching LinkedIn profiles, yet the personalization doesn't connect to the value proposition. His fix: a repeatable 2-minute personalization process:
1. Find ONE relevant trigger (not five)
2. Connect it to a problem your product solves
3. Add one line of third-party proof
4. Send

The goal is "relevant personalization" not "deep personalization." Knowing someone's dog breed is creepy. Knowing they just hired a VP of Sales is relevant.

## Post 3: Why Prospects Ghost You
**Date**: 2025
**Source**: LinkedIn

**Summary**: Three reasons prospects ghost after initial interest:
1. **Commission breath**: Prospects can sense when you're too attached to the outcome. Whenever they feel the push, they pull away.
2. **Love bombing**: Too many compliments from salespeople backfire — they seem insincere and make prospects skeptical.
3. **No big expensive problem**: Your solution has no value without a problem. And problems only matter if they matter to YOUR prospect, not to you.

**Key Insight**: "I can tell you what you want to hear — or what you need to know." Braun's philosophy is anti-persuasion. He argues 98% of sales outreach emails attempt to persuade, and that's exactly why they fail.
