## Post 1: The 4 Things That Kill Cold Email Campaigns in 2026
**Date**: 2026 (Recent)
**Source**: https://www.linkedin.com/in/alexanderberman/

**Summary**: Berman outlines four failure points for cold email in 2026:
1. **The List** — Most people start with email copy. Wrong. If your list is garbage, nothing else matters. Verify every email before sending.
2. **The Target** — One client targeting top SaaS companies (10K+ employees) saw 30% bounce rates because enterprise companies use AI-powered spam detection. Switching to 5K-10K employee companies dropped bounce rate to 0%.
3. **The Offer** — Stop using "no-brainer" language like "guaranteed 10x ROI." It looks spammy. Instead, lead with a case study featuring their competitors. Even uninterested prospects reply to see what competitors are doing.
4. **Deliverability** — In 2026, if you're not warming domains and managing DNS records, your emails hit spam. Period.

**Key Insight**: "Boring industries have empty inboxes." If everyone emails VP Sales at SaaS companies, your reply rates suffer regardless of copy quality. Target logistics, HVAC, or manufacturing and compete against zero other cold emailers.

## Post 2: The 3C Framework (Recurring Theme)
**Date**: Ongoing
**Source**: https://alexberman.com/

**Summary**: Berman's signature framework for writing cold emails:
- **Compliment**: Open with something specific about the prospect (reference their YouTube views, a recent hire, a product launch). Shows you did research.
- **Case Study**: Share a relevant result you achieved for a similar company. "We helped [COMPANY] increase revenue by 37% last quarter — want to see how?"
- **Call to Action**: End with a single, low-friction binary question. Not a calendar link. Not "let me know if you're interested." A yes/no question like "Worth a 15-minute call next week?"

**Key Insight**: The CTA should be binary — not a calendar link. Binary questions get higher response rates because they reduce cognitive load.

## Post 3: Speed-to-Lead Problem
**Date**: 2026
**Source**: LinkedIn

**Summary**: Berman argues most businesses don't have a lead quality problem — they have a speed-to-lead problem. If you don't reply to inbound leads within 5 minutes, your competitor already did. His fix:
1. Automate the first touch (SMS beats email for speed)
2. Implement a strict 5-minute rule for phone follow-ups
3. Use a CRM that pings your phone the second a lead opts in
4. Build pre-written templates for common objections

**Key Insight**: A 24-hour response delay is a "death sentence" in 2026. The window between lead and first contact should be under 5 minutes.
