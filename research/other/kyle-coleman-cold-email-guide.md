# Kyle Coleman — 2025 Cold Email Best Practices Guide

**Source**: https://www.copy.ai/blog/cold-email-best-practices-guide-2024-edition
**Author**: Kyle Coleman, CMO at Copy.ai
**Background**: 12+ years in cold email. Early employee at Looker (acquired by Google for $2.6B), CMO at Clari (10x ARR growth), now CMO at Copy.ai.

## Core Principles

Coleman's guide synthesizes a decade of practice into principles that remain constant even as tools and platforms change:

### 1. The Subject Line Rule
Keep it short (3-5 words), lowercase, and curiosity-driven. It should read like a subject line from a colleague, not a marketer. Examples:
- "quick question"
- "saw your [specific thing]"
- "[mutual connection] mentioned you"

Avoid: ALL CAPS, emojis, numbers/statistics, anything that looks like a marketing email.

### 2. The First Line Test
If your first line could be sent to any other prospect and still make sense, it's not personalized enough. The first line must pass this test: "Could this ONLY be sent to this one person?" If not, rewrite it.

### 3. The Body: Problem > Solution > Proof
Structure every cold email as:
1. Acknowledge a specific problem (1 sentence)
2. Introduce how you solve it (1 sentence)
3. Provide proof it works (1 sentence — case study or metric)

Total body: 3-5 sentences. Under 75 words. Anything longer gets skimmed or deleted.

### 4. The CTA Hierarchy
From highest to lowest conversion:
1. "Is this worth exploring?" (open-ended, low commitment)
2. "Would [specific date/time] work for a quick chat?" (specific but flexible)
3. "Here's my Calendly: [link]" (convenient but presumptuous for first touch)

Coleman recommends #1 for first emails, #2 for follow-ups, #3 only after a positive reply.

### 5. Follow-Up Cadence
- Each follow-up must add NEW value (case study, data point, insight)
- Never say "just following up" or "bumping this to the top of your inbox"
- 3-4 follow-ups maximum. After that, move to a nurture sequence.
- Spacing: Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14

### 6. AI-Synthesized Content (Not AI-Generated)
Coleman's reframe on AI in outbound: the content should be "synthesized, not generated." AI processes inputs (prospect data, company news, product info) and synthesizes a personalized email. But a human reviews, edits, and approves. The distinction matters for quality and authenticity.

Copy.ai's approach: AI does the research and drafting. Humans do the judgment and final edit. This reportedly generated $50M in pipeline on $37K of content spend.

## 2026 Benchmarks Referenced
- Average cold email reply rate: 3-4%
- Top performers: 10%+
- Email + LinkedIn combined: 15-25% reply rates
- Ideal email length: 25-75 words for first touch
- Follow-up emails: 50-100 words
