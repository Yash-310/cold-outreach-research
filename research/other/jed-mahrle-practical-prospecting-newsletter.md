## Key Content Themes

### 1. Deliverability as the #1 Priority
Mahrle's consistent message: your email copy doesn't matter if it lands in spam. His deliverability checklist:
- SPF, DKIM, DMARC records properly configured
- Use dedicated sending domains (not your primary domain)
- Warm up new domains for 2-3 weeks before sending campaigns
- Start at 10-15 emails/day, increase by 5-10 per week
- Monitor blacklists (MXToolbox, Google Postmaster Tools)
- Bounce rate must stay under 3% — above that, stop and fix your list
- Never use link tracking in cold emails (increases spam score)

### 2. Sequence Architecture
Mahrle's framework for building outbound sequences (from Mailshake collaboration):
- **Email 1 (Day 1)**: Problem-focused, 50-75 words, one question CTA
- **Email 2 (Day 3)**: New angle — share a case study or insight
- **LinkedIn touch (Day 4)**: View profile + connection request
- **Email 3 (Day 7)**: Third-party credibility (customer quote, data point)
- **Call (Day 8)**: Reference the emails — "I sent you a couple emails about X"
- **Email 4 (Day 14)**: Break-up email — "Closing the loop, seems like timing isn't right"

Each email should be independently valuable — never "just following up."

### 3. Enterprise SDR Strategies
Mahrle notes enterprise SDRs have one of the hardest jobs. His advice:
- Landing a whale meeting requires the right timing, method, and messaging — all three
- Enterprise prospects require 15-20 touches (vs 5-7 for SMB)
- Research depth must increase proportionally to deal size
- Use digital sales rooms to provide value before asking for time

### 4. Nurture Sequence Results
Mahrle shared results from nurture sequences (post-initial outreach, for prospects who didn't convert):
- Keep nurture emails even shorter (under 50 words)
- Share content they'd find genuinely useful (not your marketing content)
- One touch per month — any more frequent and it becomes annoying
- Track opens and clicks to identify re-engagement signals
- When a nurture prospect engages, move them back to an active sequence
