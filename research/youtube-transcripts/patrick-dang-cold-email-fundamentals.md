## Transcript Summary: Top Lead Generation Mistakes & How to Fix Them

### Context
Dang's content targets early-career SDRs and founders who are new to cold outreach. His strength is breaking down fundamentals that experienced practitioners skip over. This summarizes key teachings from his cold email and LinkedIn prospecting video series.

### Mistake 1: Sending Without a Clear ICP
Most people start writing emails before defining who they're writing to. Dang's process:
1. Define the company profile (industry, size, tech stack, growth stage)
2. Define the buyer persona (title, responsibilities, pain points)
3. Validate with existing customer data — who actually bought?
4. Build the list AFTER these definitions are locked

### Mistake 2: Leading With Features Instead of Problems
"We offer a platform that does X, Y, and Z" — nobody cares. Flip it: "Companies in [industry] often struggle with [specific problem]. We helped [company] solve it by [result]." The email should make the prospect feel understood, not pitched.

### Mistake 3: No Follow-Up System
Dang emphasizes that most deals happen after the 3rd-5th follow-up, not the first email. His recommended cadence:
- Day 1: Initial email
- Day 3: Follow-up #1 (add new value, don't just bump)
- Day 7: Follow-up #2 (different angle or case study)
- Day 14: Follow-up #3 (break-up style — "closing the loop")
- Parallel: LinkedIn connection request after email #1

### Key Framework: The Problem-Agitate-Solve Email Structure
1. **Problem**: Name a specific pain the prospect likely has
2. **Agitate**: Quantify the cost of inaction ("this typically costs companies like yours $X/month")
3. **Solve**: Introduce your solution through a case study, not a feature list

### LinkedIn-Specific Advice
- Optimize your profile as a landing page BEFORE sending connection requests
- Connection request message should be under 300 characters
- Never pitch in the connection request — just connect
- Follow up 24-48 hours after connection with a value-first message
- Engage with prospect's content (like, comment) for 1-2 weeks before outreach
