## Transcript Summary

### The SalesBread Method: 1 Qualified Lead Per Day

**Core Philosophy**: Volume is a trap. Most agencies send 10,000 emails to book 10 meetings. SalesBread sends 100 ultra-personalized messages to book 5-10 meetings. The math: fewer touches × dramatically higher conversion = better ROI and no burned domains.

### Step 1: Ultra-Refined List Building (Takes 1 Week)
- Analyze who's ACTUALLY buying (not who you think should buy)
- Identify common patterns in existing customers (company size, tech stack, recent events)
- Build look-alike prospect lists based on these patterns
- Verify every contact manually
- Exclude anyone who doesn't match multiple qualifying criteria

### Step 2: Human-Written Personalization
Every message references specific details:
- A prospect's recent blog post or LinkedIn content
- A product launch or company announcement
- A specific challenge common to their role/industry
- Something genuine (not forced) that creates a connection

No AI, no templates, no spintax. Each message is written by an in-house personalization expert who researches each prospect individually.

### Step 3: Multi-Channel Follow-Up
- LinkedIn connection request (with or without note — Reamer tests both)
- Email follow-up 24-48 hours after LinkedIn
- Second LinkedIn message if connected but no email reply
- Total sequence: 4-6 touches across both channels over 2-3 weeks

### Recent Episode Highlights (2026)

**LinkedIn Engagement Scraping for Prospecting**:
- Scrape engagements on relevant posts (comments, likes)
- Enrich the data to filter by ICP criteria
- Exclude competitors
- Send connection requests to engaged users
- Results: ~70% connection acceptance rate at 25 requests/day
- ~100 new ICP connections per week

**Single vs Multi-Channel Strategy**:
- Cold email alone: works for high volume, lower quality
- LinkedIn alone: works for precision targeting, lower volume
- Combined: optimal for most B2B SaaS. Email as primary, LinkedIn as warm-up/fallback
- Key: don't run both in parallel without coordination. Each channel should reference the other.

**Holiday Outreach**:
- Counter-intuitive finding: holiday periods often have HIGHER reply rates
- Fewer competitors sending = less inbox competition
- Prospects have more downtime to read and respond
- Recommendation: don't pause campaigns during holidays
